## 📝 Detailed Notes

### Introduction & The Scalability Problem

- Shivansh (Software Developer at Optum) explains that a basic app running on **localhost (4GB-6GB RAM)** might handle 10–20 requests per second, but will crash if hit with **1 million users**,.
- System design is required to bridge the gap between small-scale functionality and massive user loads.

### Defining Latency: The Response Time Metric

- **Definition:** Latency is the **Response Time**—the time elapsed between making a request and receiving a result.
- **Goal:** In any system, the objective is to **decrease latency**.
- 💡 **Analogy:** Writing names in a hotel register. If you take a long time to write one entry, your latency is high,.

### Real-World Latency Examples: YouTube & WhatsApp

- **YouTube Search Flow:**
  1. User searches a term (e.g., "System Design Course").
  2. Request hits the **Back-end API endpoint** (`/search?q=SDC`).
  3. The Back-end logic queries the **Database (DB)**.
  4. DB processes millions of videos, checking views, likes, and creator relevance (the YouTube algorithm),.
  5. A response is generated and sent back to the front-end to display the video list.
- **Performance Benchmarks:**
  - **Google Search:** Typically delivers results in ~200ms (1/5th of a second).
  - **WhatsApp:** Messages usually travel in 100ms (1/10th of a second), including blue tick/double tick updates.

### Defining Throughput: Requests Per Second (RPS)

- **Definition:** Throughput is the number of **requests a system can process** in a specific unit of time (usually per second),.
- 💡 **Analogy:** If you can write 50 entries in a hotel register per minute, your throughput is 50 per minute,.
- ⚠️ **Warning:** Systems like WhatsApp or Instagram crash when throughput exceeds the system's capacity (e.g., 1 million people searching simultaneously when the limit is lower).

### Throughput Formula & Calculations

- **Formula:** $\text{Throughput} = \frac{\text{Total Requests Processed}}{\text{Total Time Taken}}$,.
- Example: If a server handles **2,000 requests in 10 seconds**, the throughput is **200 requests per second**.

### The Coffee Shop Analogy: Latency vs. Throughput

- **Latency:** The time it takes to prepare **one coffee**.
- **Throughput:** How many coffees are made **per minute**.
- **Relationship:** To improve a coffee shop (system), you must decrease the time to make one coffee (latency) and increase the total number of coffees made (throughput),.

### Scalability: Growth Capacity

- **Definition:** The system's ability to handle increasing load by **adding resources**,.
- It is the combination of lowering latency and increasing throughput to handle growth,.

### Vertical Scaling (Scale-Up) & Its Limits

- **Definition:** Increasing the power of an **existing machine** (e.g., upgrading from 8GB RAM to 32GB RAM).
- **Pros:** Simple to manage.
- **Cons:**
  - **Hardware Limits:** You cannot have a machine with 1 million GB of RAM.
  - **Diminishing Returns:** Chipsets have physical limits regarding heat and processing speed.
- 💡 **Analogy:** Training a single waiter to be extremely fast. Eventually, he reaches his human limit (e.g., 30 seconds for a coffee) and cannot go faster.

### Horizontal Scaling (Scale-Out) & Load Balancing

- **Definition:** Adding **more machines** (servers/pods) of the same capacity to distribute the load,.
- **Pros:** Theoretically allows for **infinite scaling**.
- **Cons:**
  - **Management Overhead:** Need to manage multiple servers and salaries (resources).
  - **Complexity:** Requires a **Load Balancer** to distribute tasks and ensure **synchronization** between servers,.
- 💡 **Analogy:** Hiring 10 waiters instead of one. If 10 customers arrive, 10 waiters can serve them simultaneously in 30 seconds, whereas one waiter would take 300 seconds.

### Optimization Techniques

- **Caching:** Reduces "network hops" by storing data closer to the user, improving speed.
- **Algorithms:** Using efficient **Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA)** to reduce Time Complexity.
- **Queuing & Asynchronous Processing:** Handling time-consuming tasks in the background while the main program continues.

### Real-Time Case Studies

- **Instagram (Regional Data Centers):** Stores data in India (Mumbai) for Indian users rather than the USA to reduce latency for 50MB files.
- **Netflix & Stock Market (Auto-Scaling):**
  - **Stock Market:** High traffic only from 9:30 AM to 3:30 PM; servers scale up for this window.
  - **Netflix:** Peaks between 9 PM and 12 AM; system auto-scales to 5x or 6x capacity during these hours,.
- **Amazon/Flipkart:** During "Big Billion Days," traffic spikes 10x, requiring massive horizontal scaling.

## 🔁 Comparisons & Trade-offs

| Metric / Strategy      | Focus                            | Limitations                                            |
| :--------------------- | :------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Latency**            | **Speed** (Time for 1 request)   | Network speed, physical distance, algorithms.          |
| **Throughput**         | **Volume** (Requests per second) | CPU/RAM limits, Database lock contention.              |
| **Vertical Scaling**   | **Hardware Upgrades**            | Hard limit on hardware specs; single point of failure. |
| **Horizontal Scaling** | **Resource Addition**            | High management complexity; needs load balancing.      |

## 🎯 Interview Questions

1. **What is the difference between Latency and Throughput?**
   Latency is the time taken to process a single request (speed), while Throughput is the number of requests processed per unit of time (efficiency).
2. **When should you choose Horizontal Scaling over Vertical Scaling?**
   Choose horizontal scaling when you hit hardware limits of a single machine or when you need high availability and infinite scaling potential,.
3. **How does caching affect system metrics?**
   Caching reduces latency by decreasing network hops and speeds up throughput by reducing the load on the main database,.
4. **What is "Auto-scaling" in the context of Netflix or Amazon?**
   It is the ability of a system to automatically add or remove servers based on real-time traffic demands (e.g., scaling up during 9 PM peak hours),.
5. **Explain the formula for Throughput.**
   Throughput is calculated by dividing the total number of successfully processed requests by the total time taken to process them.

## 📌 Key Takeaways

- **Latency** = One request's time; **Throughput** = Many requests' volume.
- Always aim to **decrease Latency** and **increase Throughput**.
- **Vertical scaling** is easy but has a ceiling; **Horizontal scaling** is complex but limitless,.
- Real-world systems use **Auto-scaling** to manage predictable traffic peaks (Netflix, Stock Market).
- **Caching** and **efficient algorithms** are the first line of defense before adding more hardware.
